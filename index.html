Predicting Protein Function with Amino Acid Sequence

Recurrent Neural Net Classifier

Though proteins are essential to life, the process of discovering their function and structure is time consuming. Techniques such as x-ray crystallography and functional assays are resource-demanding and low-output compared to the high output of new-age DNA sequencing techniques. Currently, there are several popular tools available to align a novel protein sequence with known, annotated proteins in attempt to extract information about the novel sequence's origin or function. Such tools, such as BLAST, use sequence alignment to determine homology and sequence similarity to these known proteins. As DNA sequence data continues to expeditiously grow, it would be highly beneficial to match pace of protein function and structure identification with sequencing. Using natural language processing techniques, I strove to determine if there was a potential for use of artificial intelligence in rapid protein function identification away from homology or sequence alignment.

The final product model had the following neural network architecture: an embedding layer, two convolutional layers for feature extraction, a bidirectional GRU layer to avoid vanishing or exploding gradients, a single hidden layer of 120 nodes, and an output layer with ‘softmax’ activation. The final model scored approximately 90.9% on balanced accuracy score and 94.4% on f1-score.

Covid-19 Pneumonia, Viral Pneumonia, or Normal?

CNN Chest x-Ray Classifier

At the time this project was created, the world at large was in the midst of the global COVID-19 pandemic. At project release, hospitals, both rural and urban, all over the United States were undergoing extreme stress due to Covid-19 hospitalizations. Our team strove to identify a reliable and rapid way to identify Covid-19 pneumonia, using anterior-posterior and posterior-anterior chest x-rays and artificial intelligence techniques. We focused on the classification of 3 classes of A-P/P-A chest radiography: Covid-19 pneumonia, viral pneumonia, and normal presentation. Harnessing the convolutional neural network (CNN), an artificial intelligence algorithm, we produced a multi-class classification model with high predictive power – scoring approximately 93.5% on both accuracy and recall.

The final production model had the following neural network architecture: data augmentation via ImageDataGenerator, a convolutional layer for edge detection/feature extraction, and a single hidden layer of 300 nodes, and an output layer with ‘softmax’ activation.

Predicting Protein Function Using Amino Acid Sequence Classifying Reddit Posts with NLP

r/biology or r/biochemistry?

The basis of learning and scientific discovery has always started with a question; posed by those with the curiosity to gravitate toward a deeper level of understanding. As a student, in the age of the internet, something as simple as the phrasing of a google search can determine if you find the correct resources to answer a query. To the benefit of students and other curios individuals, Reddit is a main-stream forum where professionals and knowledgeable individuals gather to answer questions and discuss forum-related topics. In the field of natural sciences, there are quite a number of different branches of study and for new students or laypersons the specific topics studied by each branch can be a mystery.

I utilized web scraping, with the help of PushShift’s API, natural language processing techniques and classification algorithms to create a classification model, which helps students or curios persons determine which subreddit, either r/biology or r/biochemistry, is best to post their biology-related question. The final production model had the following attributes: CountVectorization with a curated list of stop words and a MultinomialNB estimator.
