Predicting Protein Function with Amino Acid Sequence

GitHub: Recurrent Neural Net Classifier

Though proteins are essential to life, the process of discovering their function and structure is time consuming. Techniques such as x-ray crystallography and functional assays are resource-demanding and low-output compared to the high-throughput of next-generation DNA sequencing (NGS) techniques. Currently, there are several popular tools available, which align a novel protein sequence with known, annotated sequences. Sequence alignment is used in an attempt to extract information about the novel sequence's evolutionary (homologic) origin or function. Such tools, like BLAST (Basic Local Alignment Search Tool), use sequence alignment to determine homology and sequence similarity to annotated proteins within a database. As DNA sequence data continues to expeditiously grow, it would be highly beneficial to match the pace of protein function and structure identification with NGS. Using natural language processing techniques, I strove to determine if there is a potential for use of artificial intelligence in protein function classification away from the traditional techniques of homology and sequence alignment.

The final production model had the following neural network architecture: an embedding layer, three convolutional layers for feature extraction, a bidirectional GRU layer to avoid vanishing or exploding gradients, and two hidden layers of 160 & 80 nodes, respectively. The final model scored approximately 94.3% on balanced accuracy and 95.7% on f1-score.
